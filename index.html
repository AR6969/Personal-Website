Overview

I'm Ali Rafie, a second-year law student at UC Berkeley School of Law with a passion for startups, private companies, corporate law, and helping others navigate their legal careers.

Before law school, I spent 7 years in enterprise B2B software and tech sales at Samsara, Productiv, and Axe Automation, where I was responsible for $1M+ quotas. That experience taught me how to understand what people actually need, ask the right questions, and help them achieve their goals. I loved the negotiation, the problem-solving, and figuring out how to create win-win outcomes.

My interest in the commercial side ultimately led me to pursue my JD. I bring that same approach to law. I currently serve as President of AI @ Berkeley Law, host the Legal Leaders Podcast, and serve as a Managing Editor of the Berkeley Business Law Journal. Over the past two years, I've had the privilege of interviewing many Chief Legal Officers and General Counsels. Those conversations have given me unique insight into what successful legal careers actually look like.

Services

Law School Admissions & Big Law Recruiting Consulting

I work with applicants and law students who want more than generic advice. The focus is on helping you position yourself clearly, tailor your narrative to your goals, and compete for the schools and firms you actually want.

Law School Applications Big Law Recruiting Narrative Positioning Interview Strategy

Tailored positioning for people aiming high

I was fortunate to secure both a 1L and 2L BigLaw summer offer in my top offices, and both came with diversity fellowship / DEI scholarship awards totaling $100K. I also currently mentor many 1Ls and incoming law students and genuinely enjoy helping people position themselves to land the schools, firms, and roles they want most.

Before law school, I spent 7 years in sales, which gives me a different lens than what most career offices provide. Schools often give broad advice. I work with you to tailor the story to you: your background, your goals, your work experience, your target schools, your desired firms, and how to make your narrative hit with decision-makers.

I specialize in helping people stand out. Law schools and BigLaw increasingly value maturity, judgment, and professional polish in ways that can feel closer to MBA-style evaluation than people realize. That is where positioning, messaging, and interview strategy really matter.

Core Services

My consulting is centered on two main areas. Both are highly tailored, narrative-driven, and designed around what actually moves outcomes rather than generic templates.

Admissions Strategy

Law School Applications

For law school applicants, I help craft a sharper application strategy built around your story, your strengths, and the specific schools you are targeting. That includes helping you frame your background in a way that feels authentic, compelling, and aligned with the narrative politics and culture of each individual school.

  • Personal statement and essay development, from idea shaping to line-by-line refinement.
  • Narrative positioning so your application tells one coherent story instead of reading like disconnected accomplishments.
  • School-by-school tailoring based on how different programs think about fit, culture, ambition, and professional identity.
  • Resume review, addenda strategy, and help explaining work experience, pivots, gaps, or nontraditional backgrounds the right way.
  • Application strategy focused on where you should lean in, where you should be careful, and how to present yourself with intention.
Recruiting Positioning

Big Law Recruiting

For BigLaw recruiting, I bring a deep interest in V100 / Am Law 100 firms and a strong understanding of how to position candidates based on what kind of practice they want, what office they want, and what story they need to tell. I love talking through firm strategy and helping people map themselves to the right opportunities.

  • Interview preparation and message discipline so you come across polished, thoughtful, and genuinely differentiated.
  • Firm and practice-group positioning across private equity, venture capital, private companies, startups, real estate, public company work, and capital markets.
  • Office strategy based on geography, market dynamics, and how to explain why you want a specific office in a credible way.
  • Resume, networking, and outreach strategy that reflects what firms actually value in candidates with and without prior work experience.
  • Practical advice on how to set yourself apart in a market that increasingly values maturity, commercial instincts, and real professional experience.
